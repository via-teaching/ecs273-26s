Gilead Sciences (NASDAQ:GILD) is leaning further into oncology with a sizable bet on next-generation cancer therapies, agreeing to acquire Germany-based Tubulis GmbH for up to $5 billion. The deal includes $3.15 billion in upfront cash and as much as $1.85 billion tied to milestones, with closing expected in the second quarter. Gilead (NASDAQ:GILD) shares slipped less than 1% in premarket trading, suggesting investors are balancing near-term dilution against what could be longer-term pipeline expansion.

The strategic focus here centers on antibody-drug conjugates, or ADCs, a fast-growing class of therapies designed to deliver potent cancer-killing agents directly into tumors. The space has been attracting significant capital and consolidation, including Pfizer Inc.'s $43 billion acquisition of Seagen Inc. in 2023. Tubulis brings both platform capabilities and clinical-stage programs targeting ovarian and lung cancers, alongside existing collaborations with Bristol-Myers Squibb and Gilead, which could help accelerate development timelines and broaden its oncology footprint.
Management is positioning the acquisition as a step toward building a more competitive ADC platform, complementing Gilead's existing drug Trodelvy in breast cancer. Tubulis is expected to operate as a dedicated ADC research arm, with its Munich site serving as a central hub. The move also follows a series of deals this year, including planned acquisitions of Ouro Medicines and Arcellx Inc., as Gilead appears to be accelerating efforts to strengthen its pipeline in areas such as ovarian cancer, a market that could reach $5.6 billion by 2030.
