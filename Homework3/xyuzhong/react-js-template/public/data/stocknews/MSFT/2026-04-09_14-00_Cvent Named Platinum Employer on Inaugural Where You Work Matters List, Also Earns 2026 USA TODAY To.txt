Two independent recognitions — one measuring real career outcomes based on workforce data, one driven by direct employee feedback — confirm Cvent's commitment to building meaningful careers and a people-first culture
TYSONS, Va., April 09, 2026--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Cvent, an industry-leading meetings, events, and hospitality technology provider, has earned Platinum recognition on the inaugural 2026 Where You Work Matters List, a new independent benchmark that rates how effectively major U.S. employers create real career opportunity, measured by actual workforce outcomes rather than company self-reporting. Cvent is among ~115 technology companies, out of the more than 1,750 employers assessed, to earn the overall Platinum recognition. Other Platinum-ranked organizations include Adobe, Amazon, Autodesk, DocuSign, HubSpot, LinkedIn, Microsoft, Qualtrics, SAP, and Salesforce. The company also received the 2026 USA TODAY Top Workplaces award, based on confidential employee feedback.
Together, these recognitions — one grounded in independent labor market data and one in direct employee voice — offer a compelling, multi-dimensional picture of Cvent as an employer at a time when the company is in an active growth phase and AI is reshaping the future of work.
"We've invested intentionally in building a culture where people feel supported and empowered to grow their careers," said Reggie Aggarwal, Cvent CEO and Founder. "These awards are especially meaningful because they reflect both independent workforce data and the first-hand experiences of our own people. Whether individuals are just starting out or stepping into new leadership roles, we want them to find a place at Cvent — and seeing that confirmed from these high-caliber organizations makes it all the more significant."
A New Benchmark for Employer Quality: The Where You Work Matters List
The Where You Work Matters List is powered by the American Opportunity Index, a joint project of the Schultz Family Foundation and the Burning Glass Institute, developed in partnership with the Project on Managing the Future of Work at Harvard Business School. The List rates nearly 55,000 jobs across 1,750 U.S.-based employers, using the publicly available career progression data of more than 12 million American workers. In a time of rapid change and mounting economic instability, the List highlights companies with highly effective workforce strategies that provide them with a differentiated business advantage toward innovation, productivity, and brand loyalty.
Cvent's Platinum badge — the program's highest recognition level — reflects organizational excellence across three categories: early career opportunity, job advancement and development, and long-term stability. Platinum recognition signals that Cvent is not only attracting talent but actively advancing it at every level.
"The Where You Work Matters List provides essential new transparency about how well major American employers enable workers to advance in their careers, receive leading wages and be retained in their roles," said Rajiv Chandrasekaran, Managing Director of the Schultz Family Foundation. "The List offers workers new intelligence in a job market shaken by AI advancements and economic pressures. It also allows employers to celebrate their successes and identify how they can better serve their workers across their entire organization."
To learn more and view the full 2026 rankings, visit www.whereyouworkmatters.org.
Employee Feedback Confirms the Data: USA TODAY Top Workplaces 2026
The 2026 USA TODAY Top Workplaces award, administered by Energage, honors organizations with 150 or more employees that have created exceptional, people-first cultures. This year, more than 42,000 organizations were invited to participate. Winners are selected exclusively through confidential employee surveys measuring workplace experience themes that are proven indicators of high performance, including employee voice, connection, and alignment — with no weighting given to HR policies or company submissions.
"Earning a USA TODAY Top Workplaces award is a testament to an organization's credibility and commitment to a people-first culture," said Eric Rubino, CEO of Energage. "This award, driven by real employee feedback, is more than just a recognition — it's proof that your employees believe in the organization and its leadership. Job seekers and customers look for this trusted badge of credibility and excellence."
As Cvent continues to grow and innovate, the company invites motivated professionals to explore open opportunities at cvent.com/careers.
About Cvent
Cvent is a leading meetings, events, and hospitality technology provider with more than 5,500 employees and 30,000 customers worldwide. Founded in 1999, the company delivers a comprehensive event marketing and management platform and offers a global marketplace where event professionals collaborate with venues to create engaging, impactful experiences. Cvent is headquartered in Tysons, Virginia, just outside of Washington D.C., and has additional offices around the world to support its growing global customer base. The comprehensive Cvent event marketing and management platform offers software solutions to event organizers and marketers for online event registration, venue selection, event marketing and management, virtual and onsite solutions, and attendee engagement. Cvent's suite of products automate and simplify the event management lifecycle and maximize the impact of in-person, virtual, and hybrid events. Hotels and venues use Cvent's supplier and venue solutions to win more group and corporate travel business through Cvent's sourcing platforms. Cvent solutions optimize the event management value chain and have enabled clients around the world to manage millions of meetings and events. For more information, visit www.cvent.com.

About the Burning Glass Institute
The Burning Glass Institute believes that everyone deserves meaningful work and the chance to move up. The Institute is a fully independent, nonprofit data laboratory that develops and analyzes novel datasets to construct models, metrics, and benchmarks that boost economic mobility, drive worker and community prosperity, and improve how talent and opportunity connect. Working at the intersection of the future of work and the future of learning, the Institute partners with companies, educational institutions, communities, government agencies, and philanthropies to support efforts that expand talent pipelines, advance worker mobility, and improve student outcomes. Founded in 2022 and building on a legacy of breakthrough innovation in labor market analytics, the Institute has emerged as a trusted voice bridging industry, educators, policymakers, and workers. Learn more at burningglassinstitute.org.
