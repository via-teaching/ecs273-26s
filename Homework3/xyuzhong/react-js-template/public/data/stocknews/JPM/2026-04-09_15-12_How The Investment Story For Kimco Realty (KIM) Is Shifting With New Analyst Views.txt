Kimco Realty’s latest analyst update focuses on small adjustments to fair value, with the model price target moving from US$24.85 to US$24.88. Research commentary around the name reflects a mix of higher and lower targets across the low to high US$20s, often tied to sector views on retail REITs and shopping centers rather than company specific shifts alone. As you read on, you will see how these price target tweaks fit into a broader narrative you can track over time.

Piper Sandler lifted its Kimco Realty target to US$28 from US$26 and kept an Overweight view, pointing to potential benefits from buybacks when excess free cash flow is available and leverage is kept in check.
JPMorgan increased its target to US$25 from US$23 and maintained a Neutral stance after updating its model, suggesting that recent information supported a slightly higher valuation range.
BofA raised its target to US$24 from US$22, also with a Neutral rating, following Q4 updates across retail REITs, which fed into refreshed assumptions for Kimco’s earnings path.
Mizuho cut its target to US$21 from US$23 and kept a Neutral rating after downgrading the shopping center REIT group, citing sector wide concerns that fundamentals and occupancy levels may already be near a peak.
Barclays reduced its target to US$25 from US$27 while staying Overweight, and flagged a preference for apartments, storage, and single family rentals over retail within its broader 2026 REIT outlook.

Kimco Realty completed a share repurchase program announced on February 15, 2018, buying back 8,100,000 shares (1.64% of its stock) for a total of US$133.71 million.
The company reported activity under a newer buyback plan announced on November 4, 2025, with 3,080,169 shares (0.45% of its stock) repurchased for US$61.49 million.
Kimco issued full year 2026 earnings guidance, with net income available to common shareholders projected in a range of US$0.80 to US$0.84 per share.
Fair value moved from US$24.85 to US$24.88 in the updated model.
Revenue growth was adjusted from 3.40% to 3.25%.
Net profit margin shifted from 25.64% to 25.96%.
Future P/E changed from 34.35x to 34.18x.
The discount rate moved from 7.99% to 8.06%.
