NetApp’s latest valuation work keeps the core fair value estimate steady at US$117.93, while recent Street price targets span roughly US$88 to US$120 and include a fresh initiation at US$128. That spread lines up with current analyst debate, where some see long term upside tied to flash and public cloud growth and others focus on margin pressure and higher memory costs when trimming targets toward the high US$80s to low US$110s. As you read on, you will see how to track these shifting targets and what they may mean for your own view on NetApp.
Analyst Price Targets don't always capture the full story. Head over to our Company Report to find new ways to value NetApp.
Goldman Sachs set one of the higher targets at US$128 when it initiated coverage with a Buy rating, highlighting NetApp's position in all flash storage and exposure to faster growing media and data types.
Barclays kept an Overweight rating even after trimming its target to US$120 from US$134, indicating confidence in the equity case despite pointing to weaker product margins in the latest quarter.
BofA, with a Neutral rating and a reduced target of US$115 from US$129, pointed to mix shift toward flash and public cloud revenue as a supportive long term element against current cost pressures.
Morgan Stanley cut its target to US$88 from US$89 and maintains an Underweight rating, flagging higher memory costs and limited pre purchase activity as risks to product gross margins and demand in FY27.
Citi and UBS both sit at Neutral with targets of US$114 and US$113 respectively, reflecting mixed end demand commentary and a more balanced view on execution versus margin and component cost risks.

NetApp and Commvault formed a data protection alliance that combines NetApp’s AI driven ransomware detection with Commvault’s protection and recovery tools to support automated, large scale cyber recovery across on premises and cloud environments.
NetApp is adding Elastio’s Provable Recovery Control to its Ransomware Resilience Service to inspect ONTAP snapshots, aiming to identify clean recovery points and address ransomware that may sit in backups and snapshots.
NetApp launched next generation EF50 and EF80 EF Series storage systems, targeting AI, high performance computing and transactional database workloads, with stated read throughput over 110 GBps, write throughput of 55 GBps and 1.5 PB of storage in a 2U form factor.
NetApp announced the NetApp AI Data Engine, a unified AI data platform stack co engineered with NVIDIA, with an initial rollout to lighthouse customers and broader availability expected in early summer.
