Health insurers are seeing a sharp reset in sentiment after US regulators finalized a higher Medicare Advantage payment update than initially proposed, a shift that could begin to stabilize earnings expectations across the group. The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services confirmed a 2.48% rate increase for 2027, improving meaningfully from the near-flat proposal outlined in January that had weighed heavily on the sector. The policy continues to dictate how more than half a trillion dollars flows through private plans, with CMS indicating the change could result in over $13 billion in additional payments next year, offering some relief as insurers contend with elevated medical costs.

The market reaction was immediate and telling. UnitedHealth Group (NYSE:UNH) rose 6.6% in premarket trading, Humana (NYSE:HUM) climbed 11%, and CVS Health (NYSE:CVS) advanced 5.8%, suggesting investors may be recalibrating expectations after the earlier selloff tied to the January proposal. That earlier framework, which effectively held rates flat, had erased nearly $100 billion in market value across Medicare-focused insurers, as companies flagged the difficulty of absorbing higher utilization without corresponding reimbursement support. Into the final decision, expectations had centered on a modest improvement, with analysts pointing to a roughly 1 to 1.5 percentage point increase as potentially sufficient.
What stands out now is not just the higher rate, but the broader policy tone. While the increase itself is not outsized in isolation, it could allow insurers to rebuild margins if cost discipline and benefit adjustments continue. At the same time, CMS opted to defer certain changes to the risk adjustment model, a decision that may signal a more measured regulatory approach after industry pushback. Taken together, the combination of improved funding visibility and delayed structural changes could set up a more constructive earnings trajectory over the next couple of years, even as regulators continue trying to balance near-term program stability with longer-term sustainability.
