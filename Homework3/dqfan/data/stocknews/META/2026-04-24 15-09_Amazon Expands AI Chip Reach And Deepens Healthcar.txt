Amazon.com (NasdaqGS:AMZN) and Meta announced a large-scale partnership to deploy AWS Graviton custom silicon for Meta's workloads.
The agreement expands the reach of Amazon's in house chip ecosystem across AI and cloud infrastructure.
Separately, Amazon launched a nationwide GLP-1 weight loss drug program through One Medical and Amazon Pharmacy.
The new healthcare offering integrates telehealth, prescriptions, and fulfillment for obesity treatments in the US.
At a share price of $255.08, Amazon.com (NasdaqGS:AMZN) sits after a period of strong recent performance, with the stock up 20.5% over the past month and 35.0% over the past year. Over three years the share price return is 141.9%, while the five year return stands at 47.1%, highlighting how much timing has mattered for different entry points.
The new chip partnership and GLP-1 program extend Amazon’s reach into AI infrastructure and healthcare services, both areas that attract significant attention from investors. For anyone tracking Amazon, these moves add additional context to the existing e commerce and cloud story and may influence how you think about the mix of businesses behind the current share price.

📰 Beyond the headline: 1 risk and 3 things going right for Amazon.com that every investor should see.
The Meta deal pushes Amazon deeper into custom-silicon and AI-infrastructure services, since Meta is set to deploy tens of millions of Graviton cores for agentic AI workloads. That helps validate AWS’s in-house chips relative to offerings from Nvidia, Intel and AMD, and reinforces Amazon’s pitch around price performance and energy efficiency for large AI customers. At the same time, the nationwide GLP-1 program through One Medical and Amazon Pharmacy gives Amazon a more visible role in primary care and obesity treatment. It combines telehealth, prescriptions and home delivery in a single funnel. For you as an investor, both moves expand the set of high-value workloads and services that sit on top of Amazon’s existing logistics and cloud infrastructure, but they also deepen exposure to regulated areas like healthcare and large-capital-spend data centers.
The expanded Graviton relationship with Meta supports the existing narrative that custom chips and AI workloads can be long-term drivers for AWS, building on recent AI cloud commitments from partners like Anthropic and OpenAI.
Both the Meta partnership and the GLP-1 rollout add to already heavy capital-expenditure and regulatory commitments. This could test assumptions in the narrative around margin expansion and the pace at which new bets translate into earnings.
The narrative focuses heavily on cloud, AI and satellites. The GLP-1 program adds a new healthcare layer that may not yet be fully reflected in expectations for how much Amazon’s medical services and pharmacy offerings contribute over time.
