In recent weeks, McDonald’s has rolled out a refreshed U.S. value push, including sub-US$3 menu items, a US$4 breakfast deal, and a revamped low-priced beverage lineup, to appeal to cost-conscious diners amid weaker consumer sentiment and higher living costs.
These changes highlight how McDonald’s is using pricing, menu innovation, and specialty drinks to balance customer traffic needs against margin pressure as inflation and fuel costs weigh on household budgets.

To own McDonald’s, you need to believe it can keep using its scale, brand and digital ecosystem to defend traffic and margins even when consumers are under pressure. The latest US value push looks directionally aligned with that story, but does not materially change the near term focus on same store sales resilience or the key risk from sustained margin pressure if input costs stay elevated while pricing remains constrained.
The refreshed McValue menu with items under US$3 and a US$4 breakfast deal is the clearest link to current concerns about weaker low income traffic and softer sentiment. It sits alongside specialty beverages like new Refreshers and crafted sodas, which relate directly to the existing catalyst around menu innovation as a tool to support comparable sales and protect restaurant level profitability.
