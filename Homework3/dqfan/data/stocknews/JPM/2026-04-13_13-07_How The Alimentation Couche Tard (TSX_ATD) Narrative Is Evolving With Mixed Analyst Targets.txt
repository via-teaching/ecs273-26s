JPMorgan recently began coverage with a bullish stance, which adds another supportive voice to the existing research and signals confidence in the company’s execution and longer term growth prospects.
Earlier in the year, TD Securities set a round-number target of C$100, while BMO Capital and Stifel also lifted their targets, reflecting constructive views on where the shares could trade relative to current fundamentals.
On 19 March, Scotiabank, CIBC and Stifel each trimmed their price targets by C$3 to C$4, suggesting some caution around how much upside is left at recent levels.
BNP Paribas initiated coverage in January with a neutral stance, which may signal concerns about valuation or a preference to see further proof of execution before taking a more positive view.
