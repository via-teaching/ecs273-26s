DT Midstream’s updated blended fair value now sits at $142.71, a small move from $141.57 that still caught the attention of analysts watching for shifts in expectations. Recent research highlights a mix of optimism and caution, as higher targets into the US$150s are weighed against questions about valuation, growth capex, and how much of the shadow backlog will translate into committed projects. Read on to see what is driving these changing views and how you can keep track of this evolving narrative.
Analyst Price Targets don't always capture the full story. Head over to our Company Report to find new ways to value DT Midstream.
Citi, BofA, Wells Fargo and UBS all lifted price targets into the US$150s, pointing to a robust organic and shadow backlog, a capital outlook of US$3.4b and what they view as attractive opportunities in gas focused projects.
Citi highlighted management commentary that the opportunity backlog is multiples of the US$3.4b capital outlook and that the existing outlook is already about 50% sanctioned, which these analysts see as supporting longer term growth projects.
Wells Fargo and Jefferies pointed to DT Midstream's smaller size and a US$7.5b gross opportunity set as giving the company more torque to potential new projects compared with some peers in the midstream space.
JPMorgan, Barclays and Mizuho all moved targets higher into the US$120s and US$140s, suggesting that, even where ratings stay Neutral or Equal Weight, updated models still support higher valuation anchors than before.
Stifel shifted to a Hold rating with a US$137 target, citing valuation as a key constraint and flagging that the shares trade at more than 14x the firm's 2027 EBITDA estimate.
Jefferies acknowledged a US$3.4b five year growth capex plan but noted this was below its best case view, which the firm linked to recent share underperformance as some investors reassessed how much of the larger US$7.5b opportunity set will become committed projects.
