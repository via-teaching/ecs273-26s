Simon Property Group’s latest fair value estimate has been updated to US$208.55 from US$206.30, a modest move that keeps the price target broadly in line with prior assumptions. This tweak sits against a backdrop of mixed analyst views, where some highlight healthier mall metrics and others focus on interest costs and more cautious valuation stances after the recent run in mall REITs. As you read on, you will see how these shifting inputs shape the evolving narrative around Simon and what to watch next in the research.
Analyst Price Targets don't always capture the full story. Head over to our Company Report to find new ways to value Simon Property Group.
BofA, Ladenburg and Truist have all lifted price targets in recent months, pointing to healthier mall operating metrics and better than previously projected net operating income at Simon Property Group.
BofA highlights what it calls the healthiest backdrop for malls in years and sees Simon’s valuation as still below levels seen in prior constructive periods. It views this as supportive for its higher targets of US$219 and then US$225.
Ladenburg’s move from US$225 to US$250 reflects its view that mall stocks are being re rated, with Simon seen as a key beneficiary of that reassessment.
Truist’s increase to US$196 comes alongside higher funds from operations estimates, tied to tenant sales, shopper traffic and tenant demand that the firm describes as trending positively.
Truist balances its more constructive view on income with higher interest expense, which it flags as a partial offset to the stronger operating trends.
Evercore ISI most recently lowered its price target after previously lifting it to US$200, signaling a more cautious stance on valuation even as it maintains an In Line rating on the shares.

Simon Property Group announced that long time Chief Executive Officer David Simon passed away on March 22, 2026 at age 64 after a battle with cancer, and the Board appointed Eli Simon as CEO effective March 23, 2026, with Eli continuing as Chief Operating Officer and Director.
The Board authorized a new share repurchase program on February 5, 2026, allowing the company to repurchase up to US$2.0b of its common stock through February 29, 2028.
Under the prior buyback announced on February 8, 2024, Simon repurchased a total of 1,638,982 shares for US$300m between October 1, 2025 and February 5, 2026.
At the Citi Global Property CEO Conference, Eli Simon said the company is seeking acquisitions and highlighted redevelopment, densification and mixed use additions, including a planned redevelopment of the former Neiman Marcus space at Copley Place in Boston, with phased openings starting in 2028.
