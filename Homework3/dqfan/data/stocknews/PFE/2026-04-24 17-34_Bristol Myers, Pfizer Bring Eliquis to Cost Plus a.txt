Mark Cuban's push to reshape drug pricing is picking up momentum as Bristol Myers Squibb (NYSE:BMY) and Pfizer (NYSE:PFE) move to bring Eliquis onto the Mark Cuban Cost Plus Drug Company platform starting Monday. The blockbuster blood thinner, Eliquis, will be offered at $345 for a 30-day supply, a price that aligns with what the drugmakers already list through their own patient support channel. The agreement could signal a broader willingness from large pharmaceutical players to experiment with alternative distribution models, particularly those that aim to simplify pricing and improve transparency.

Eliquis remains the most widely prescribed oral blood thinner in the US and continues to generate billions of dollars in annual sales, even as it moves closer to eventual generic competition. Its addition to Cuban's platform could make it one of the most prominent brand-name drugs available through the service, which until now has largely focused on generics. The shift comes as pharmaceutical companies increasingly look to reach patients directly, potentially expanding access for those paying out of pocket or lacking insurance while also sidestepping the rebates and fees tied to traditional pharmacy benefit managers.
Launched in early 2021, Mark Cuban Cost Plus Drug Company has built its model around mail-order medications sold at transparent prices, typically applying a fixed markup over acquisition costs. Bringing a high-demand branded therapy like Eliquis into that framework could test how far the model can extend beyond generics, especially as more companies explore direct-to-consumer strategies alongside existing retail channels. Over time, this approach could play a role in reshaping how certain high-volume drugs are priced and distributed across the US market.
