Semiconductor stocks rallied on Friday, with MaxLinear (NASDAQ:MXL) and Intel (NASDAQ:INTC) at the center of the move as investors bet that AI-related demand could keep supporting chip spending, according to a Friday results release. Nvidia (NASDAQ:NVDA) also joined the advance.
MaxLinear (NASDAQ:MXL) jumped after posting first-quarter results and lifting its 2026 outlook. The company pointed to stronger demand in its optical data center business, which is tied to AI infrastructure, while analysts at Needham and Roth Capital both raised their ratings and price targets on the stock. Nvidia (NASDAQ:NVDA) was among several large chip names that moved higher.

Super Micro (SMCI) and AMD (NASDAQ:AMD) also rose 8% on Friday.
Intel (NASDAQ:INTC) also climbed sharply after its quarterly report and outlook topped expectations. The company said it now sees second-quarter revenue of $13.8 billion to $14.8 billion, above Wall Street's forecast, and adjusted earnings of 20 cents a share versus estimates of 9 cents.
The broader rally spread to other Nvidia (NASDAQ:NVDA) and Intel (NASDAQ:INTC) suppliers and peers. AMD, Arm, Qualcomm, Micron and Sandisk all gained, while Microsoft and Meta Platforms also rose as investors added to AI-linked names.
