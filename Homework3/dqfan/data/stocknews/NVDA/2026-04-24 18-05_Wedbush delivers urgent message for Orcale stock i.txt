Oracle (NYSE:ORCL) drew a bullish start from Wedbush, which began coverage with an Outperform rating and a $225 price target, saying the software group may be entering a bigger shift as it leans into AI infrastructure and cloud demand. Oracle's share price was not given, but the call adds to rising attention around its AI strategy.
Oracle's case rests on partnerships with OpenAI and Nvidia (NVDA), along with work tied to the Stargate project. Wedbush said Oracle's cloud platform could gain from demand for high-performance computing as customers build larger AI systems and move more workloads online.

Oracle is also leaning on its data business. The firm pointed to Oracle AI Database 26ai and a multi-cloud approach that puts its technology inside rival ecosystems, while multi-cloud database revenue rose 531% in the fiscal third quarter of 2026.
Oracle's heavy spending has drawn debate, but Wedbush argued the capex is backed by contracts rather than speculation. Oracle's remaining performance obligations reached $553 billion, giving the company a capex-to-RPO ratio of about 9%, far below the group average of roughly 45.6%.
