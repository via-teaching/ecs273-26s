Cognition AI Inc. appears to be moving back into the spotlight as investor appetite for AI coding platforms continues to build. The startup is in early-stage discussions to raise a new funding round that could value the company at around $25 billion, more than doubling its previous valuation, according to people familiar with the matter. The company is reportedly targeting hundreds of millions of dollars or more, though negotiations are ongoing and terms could still shift. The timing comes shortly after SpaceX secured rights to acquire rival Cursor for $60 billion, a development that may be reinforcing investor conviction across the AI software development space.

While Cognition's fundraising process began prior to the Cursor announcement, interest from investors has increased following the deal, suggesting capital could be rotating more aggressively into companies building AI tools for developers. President Russell Kaplan indicated that the company has seen a rise in inbound attention from both customers and prospective employees, pointing to potential momentum at a critical stage. Founded in 2023, Cognition focuses on enterprise customers and offers its flagship product Devin, which it has described as an AI software engineer. The company lists clients such as Microsoft and Anduril Industries, reflecting early traction within commercial and defense-related applications.
Cognition's recent trajectory highlights how quickly the category is evolving. The company was generating around $73 million in annual recurring revenue as of last June and later raised $400 million at a $10.2 billion valuation, underscoring strong investor backing. It also moved to acquire the remaining assets and intellectual property of Windsurf after Alphabet Inc. (NASDAQ:GOOG) hired the startup's co-founders, signaling competitive intensity across the sector. At the same time, Cognition continues to operate in a crowded field that includes major AI model developers such as OpenAI and Anthropic, both of which are expanding their presence in tools designed for software engineers.
