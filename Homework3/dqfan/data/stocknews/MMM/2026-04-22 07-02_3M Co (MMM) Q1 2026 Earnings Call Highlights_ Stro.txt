3M Co (NYSE:MMM) delivered solid operating performance in Q1 with earnings per share of $2.14, up mid-teens versus last year.
Operating margin increased by 30 basis points to 23.8%, and free cash flow was over $500 million, up double digits.
The company returned $2.4 billion to shareholders, including $400 million in dividends and $2 billion of share repurchases.
3M Co (NYSE:MMM) introduced AI tools to drive growth, reduce churn, and automate manual work, enhancing sales effectiveness.
The pace of new product introductions is accelerating, with 84 new products launched in Q1, up 35% versus last year, and on pace to launch 350 in 2026.
Organic growth was light at 1.2%, driven by pockets of macro pressure.
The Consumer business group did not see the expected recovery in the US market, resulting in organic sales down 1%.
The electronics market experienced softness in consumer electronics due to industry-wide memory chip issues.
The automotive market was soft, with global build rates down about 3% overall, and 10% in China.
3M Co (NYSE:MMM) faced approximately $145 million of tariff impact, stranded costs, and investments.
Q: Can you provide more perspective on the pre-buy and backlog numbers? How much additional price is embedded in your organic growth forecast? A: William Brown, CEO: We had very good orders in Q1, up double digits. While we're not typically a backlog-driven business, backlog was strong and continues to build into Q2. We do a price increase every year on April 1, which may have led to some pre-buying. For the year, we expect price to contribute around 1.3 points to growth, including an extra 50 basis points from oil-related price increases. Anurag Maheshwari, CFO: We have about 75% of our revenue from book and ship, but the backlog provides 400-500 basis points of additional coverage as we enter the quarter, which is significant for growth acceleration.
Q: Are customer inventories low, and is there a restock occurring? A: William Brown, CEO: In the Safety and Industrial business group, distribution inventory is relatively normal, maybe a bit below typical levels. On the Consumer side, inventory is about normalized at around 13 weeks of supply. Overall, inventory levels are fairly normal, with some areas slightly light.
Q: Can you provide more detail on the second quarter dynamics and EPS growth expectations? A: Anurag Maheshwari, CFO: We expect organic growth in Q2 to be higher than 3%, with all business groups accelerating. Margins are expected to be solid at about 24.5%, with good EPS flow-through. We anticipate more than $0.05 EPS growth in Q2, leading to $0.30-plus EPS growth for the first half. The contingency for the second half depends on macro conditions and oil prices.
Q: What is the impact of oil prices on your business, and how are you managing this risk? A: Anurag Maheshwari, CFO: We have about $6 billion in raw material spend, with a third based on polychemicals. We expect $125 million in cost increases, which we are offsetting with pricing. William Brown, CEO: We are implementing price increases to cover these costs, expecting about 50 basis points of uplift from oil-based exposure. The impact on the macro economy and consumer spending is still unfolding.
Q: Can you discuss the progress and impact of the commercial excellence program in Transportation and Electronics? A: William Brown, CEO: The team is making good progress, following the successful model in Safety and Industrial. They are improving sales force effectiveness, pricing discipline, and targeting attrition with predictive AI models. We expect improvements in TEBG from these efforts in the back half of the year.
