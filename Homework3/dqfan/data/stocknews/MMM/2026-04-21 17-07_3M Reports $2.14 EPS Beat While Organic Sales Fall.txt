3M (NYSE:MMM) reported a first-quarter earnings beat that could signal early traction in its ongoing transformation, even as the broader demand backdrop remains uneven. Adjusted earnings came in at $2.14 per share, ahead of the $2 consensus estimate, while both sales and adjusted operating income margin were broadly in line with expectations. That said, organic sales declined 1.4%, suggesting that underlying demand conditions across industrial markets may still be soft despite the headline stability in reported revenue.

The quarter arrives as Chief Executive Officer Bill Brown continues to reposition the business toward higher-growth opportunities while simplifying operations. The company has been leaning into product innovation, highlighting technologies such as improved interconnectors for data centers and new heat-dissipation solutions for electrical systems, areas that could support incremental growth over time. At the same time, 3M is expanding its exposure to the data center market, including plans to double manufacturing capacity for its Expanded Beam Optical interconnect technology, which may gradually increase its presence in high-speed infrastructure segments.
Beyond internal execution, portfolio actions remain active, including a $1.95 billion deal with Bain Capital to acquire Madison Fire & Rescue, where 3M contributes its Scott Safety unit and retains a 50.1% stake. However, legacy liabilities continue to weigh on the outlook, with about $3.4 billion in settlement payments made in 2025 tied to litigation over so-called forever chemicals and combat earplugs, and further obligations still outstanding. Shares slipped 1.3% in premarket trading, adding to a 5.4% decline year-to-date through Monday's close and trailing the S&P 500 (SPY). Management nonetheless reaffirmed full-year guidance for adjusted earnings of $8.50 to $8.70 per share and roughly 4% sales growth, indicating confidence that the turnaround could continue to build momentum.
