First-quarter 2026 presented Internet stocks with a turbulent yet opportunity-rich landscape, as the sector navigated geopolitical tensions while underlying artificial intelligence monetization trends gained meaningful momentum. Resilient digital advertising demand, accelerating enterprise AI adoption and a supportive monetary policy environment in the first quarter are likely to have laid the groundwork for improving sentiment heading into the second quarter.Drawing on our proprietary research and market insight, we’ve identified four Internet stocks — Block XYZ, Arista Networks ANET, Meta Platforms META and Alphabet GOOGL — that appear well-positioned to beat earnings estimates this season.
The artificial intelligence buildout remained the sector's defining catalyst during the first quarter. Hyperscalers reaffirmed aggressive plans, with Amazon, Alphabet, Meta and Microsoft collectively expected to deploy roughly $720 billion in 2026 capex to strengthen AI leadership. Meta alone reiterated a $115-$135 billion capex plan dedicated to compute, data centers and AI talent. Generative AI tools transformed digital advertising workflows, with Gemini-powered Performance Max and agentic advertising platforms accelerating adoption, while Amazon Advertising's display and sponsored-ad revenues expanded at a 24.7% annualized pace, underscoring the monetization runway ahead.Digital advertising markets demonstrated encouraging resilience during the quarter, supported by improved AI-driven targeting and creative automation. Facebook CPMs firmed approximately 8% as advertisers competed for higher-intent inventory, and AI Mode in Search unlocked new ad formats bridging inspiration and conversion. Social commerce expansion, creator-led commerce on YouTube and chatbot-enabled funnels further reinforced tailwinds for advertising-dependent players.The Federal Reserve's policy stance remained supportive through the quarter, with the federal funds rate holding at 3.5-3.75%, and a positively sloped yield curve, steepening to 51 basis points by quarter-end, signaling expectations for continued economic expansion.The quarter also brought notable headwinds. Tensions tied to the geopolitical conflict, rising oil prices, and renewed inflation concerns weighed on growth stocks, while fears that AI could disrupt traditional software business models triggered a sharp selloff. Meta dropped 13.3%, Amazon fell 9.8%, and Alphabet declined more than 8% during the quarter. Tariff discussions and scrutiny of hyperscaler returns added to the cautious tone. Cost discipline emerged as another first-quarter theme, with Meta trimming roughly 10% of its workforce, affecting nearly 8,000 positions, and Microsoft extending voluntary buyouts to approximately 7% of U.S. employees. These moves signaled a focus on operating leverage as AI infrastructure spending scaled.Overall, first-quarter 2026 marked a transitional period for Internet stocks, balancing near-term pressures against powerful secular drivers. With AI monetization deepening, digital advertising strengthening, hyperscaler capex commitments intact, and valuations resetting to attractive levels, the sector appears well-positioned for improved momentum ahead.
