The region-wide total is 57% lower than before the outbreak of the Iran war, analysts including Daan Struyven said in an April 23 note. A possible restoration would likely take “a few months,” a scenario based on a full-and-safe reopening of the Strait of Hormuz and no renewed strikes, they said.
The global oil market is under increasing strain as the US and Israeli war against Iran heads toward a third month. Traffic through the Strait of Hormuz — which links the Persian Gulf to global markets — remains all but frozen, as both Tehran and Washington seek to impose blockades of ships. Global crude benchmark Brent is up for a fifth day, set for a weekly gain of 17%.
“The longer the strait is effectively closed, the longer curtailments last, the slower the production recovery is likely to be,” the analysts said.
Brent futures traded near $106 a barrel on Friday. That’s up from less than $73 a barrel before the war erupted, but below the conflict-high of almost $120.
