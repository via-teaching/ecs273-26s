UnitedHealth Group (NYSE:UNH) isseeinga sharp shift in sentiment as its shares move toward their strongest week in seven months, driven by a more than 10% rally that accelerated on Tuesday following a surprise Medicare update. The US government finalized a 2.48% increase in payments for Medicare Advantage plans in 2027, a notable change from its earlier proposal that rates would remain largely unchanged. The adjustment is being viewed as a potential near-term support for health insurers that have been under pressure from rising medical costs and tighter funding dynamics.

Even with that backdrop, skepticism remains in parts of the market. Baird analyst Michael Ha maintained his underperform rating on UnitedHealth, standing as the only sell-equivalent voice after HSBC's Sidharth Sahoo upgraded the shares to hold on the same day. Ha indicated that while the payment increase could ease some concerns around earnings growth, it may only act as a temporary offset rather than a full resolution. He also pointed out that companies with meaningful exposure to value-based care models, such as UnitedHealth, may still be facing ongoing structural pressures.
The broader setup helps explain the divergence in views. Medicare Advantage has been a key driver of insurer growth for more than a decade, but profitability has come under strain as costs increase and government support tightens. UnitedHealth recently projected a decline in 2026 revenue, which could mark its first annual contraction in more than three decades, alongside expectations for shrinking enrollment across commercial plans, Medicare, and Medicaid. While the stock remains down about 7% year to date and more than 50% below its 2024 peak, Wall Street sentiment is still largely constructive, with 22 of 31 analysts assigning buy ratings and an average 12-month price target suggesting roughly 17% upside from current levels.
