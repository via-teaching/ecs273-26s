The consensus analyst fair value estimate for QUALCOMM has shifted from US$154.93 to US$168.50, providing an updated reference point for how the Street is currently framing the stock. That move reflects a tug of war between bullish firms raising targets on AI, automotive, and IoT potential, and bearish voices focusing on smartphone maturity and the well‑flagged loss of Apple business. Several firms, including Argus, Baird, Morgan Stanley, Deutsche Bank, Benchmark, Cantor Fitzgerald, Susquehanna, TD Cowen, UBS, JPMorgan, Wells Fargo, Citi and Barclays, recently raised Qualcomm price targets by US$10 to US$123, which supports the higher consensus fair value around US$168.50. Wells Fargo highlights Qualcomm's data center plans and AI inference opportunity, citing a potential US$5b to US$7b annual revenue contribution in later years as underappreciated in current valuations. Loop Capital sees Qualcomm's diversification into Automotive and IoT as important for the long term, pointing to a future mix where these segments could be comparable in scale to Handsets. BofA and Seaport Research focus on handset maturity and rising memory costs, with Seaport assigning a US$100 price target and BofA flagging the well known US$7b to US$8b loss of Apple business as a risk to earnings quality. Bernstein, Daiwa, Morgan Stanley and BNP Paribas emphasize concerns about smartphone demand and potential share loss, which feeds into cautious views on execution and growth relative to current valuation levels. Analyst reports indicate OpenAI is working with Qualcomm on smartphone processors, increasing attention on Qualcomm's role in on-device AI for future handsets. AMD, Arm and Qualcomm committed US$60m to autonomous driving startup Wayve, highlighting Qualcomm's exposure to AI and automotive computing alongside other major chipmakers. The U.S. Commerce Department withdrew a draft rule that would have revised AI chip export controls, a policy step affecting several U.S. chipmakers including Qualcomm. Qt Group announced a collaboration with Qualcomm to simplify building Edge AI devices for factories, using Qualcomm's Dragonwing IQ IoT processors and Qualcomm Linux as an industrial AI platform. The fair value estimate has been adjusted from US$154.93 to US$168.50. The long term annual revenue growth assumption has been updated from 3.00% to 3.14%. The assumed net profit margin has been revised from 23.19% to 22.58%. The future P/E multiple assumption has been revised from 18.25x to 19.37x. The assumed discount rate has been changed from 11.01% to 10.78%.
