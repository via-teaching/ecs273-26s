Shares ofEli Lilly(LLY+3.30%)rose this past week after the medicine maker reported skyrocketing profits fueled by sales of its blockbuster weight loss drugs. Image source: Getty Images. Eli Lilly's revenue surged 56% year over year to $19.8 billion in the first quarter. Thehealthcaregiant has been working with regulators to lower prices for several of its products. But these discounts were more than offset by volume gains. In all, realized prices declined by 13%, while volume climbed 65%. The company's massively popular GLP-1 drugs Mounjaro and Zepbound were particularly powerful growth drivers, with sales rising 125% and 80%, respectively, to $8.7 billion and $4.2 billion.
