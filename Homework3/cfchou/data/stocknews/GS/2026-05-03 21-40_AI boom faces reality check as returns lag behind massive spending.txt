Title: AI boom faces reality check as returns lag behind massive spending
Date: 2026-05-03 21:40
URL: https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/articles/ai-boom-faces-reality-check-214057055.html?.tsrc=rss
Content:
Investing.com -- A new report by Goldman Sachs suggests that while artificial intelligence (AI) adoption is accelerating rapidly, the financial benefits for most companies remain elusive, raising concerns about the sustainability of current investment levels. Two years after questioning whether generative AI was attracting “too much spend and too little benefit,” analysts now say the gap between investment and returns persists. Despite tens of billions of dollars poured into AI, as many as 95% of companies are seeing little to no return on investment, highlighting a growing disconnect between expectations and real-world outcomes. The report notes that AI adoption among consumers has exceeded expectations, with usage growing faster than earlier technologies like the internet or personal computers. However, this success has not translated into enterprise profitability. Most users rely on free AI tools, limiting revenue generation and reinforcing the importance of enterprise adoption for long-term economic impact. Within companies, results remain mixed. While executives tout productivity gains, many employees report minimal time savings, and a large majority of organizations still lack meaningful, value-driving AI use cases. One of the clearest trends is the uneven distribution of profits across the AI ecosystem. Semiconductor companies have captured the bulk of financial gains, while enterprises, model developers, and cloud providers struggle to justify their heavy spending. Analysts warn this imbalance is “unsustainable” unless returns improve higher up the value chain. At the same time, major tech firms continue to ramp up spending on AI infrastructure despite weaker stock performance, driven largely by fear of missing out (FOMO) in the competitive race. Analysts argue that the key to unlocking AI’s potential lies not in better models alone, but in improving data organization and system orchestration within companies. Without these foundations, AI deployments risk remaining costly and inefficient. They also caution companies against rushing into AI investments without clear long-term strategies, warning that early adopters may bear high costs without reaping proportional rewards.
