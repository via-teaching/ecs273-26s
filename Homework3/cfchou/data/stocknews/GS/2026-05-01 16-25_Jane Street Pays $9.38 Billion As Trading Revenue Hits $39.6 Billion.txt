Title: Jane Street Pays $9.38 Billion As Trading Revenue Hits $39.6 Billion

This article first appeared onGuruFocus. Jane Street Group's rise hasbecomeone of Wall Street's most lucrative stories for the people inside the firm. The market maker paid out $9.38 billion in compensation last year, more than double the amount in 2024, according to people familiar with the matter cited by Bloomberg. On a per-employee basis, that works out to an average of $2.68 million, almost seven times as much as rival Goldman Sachs Group (NYSE:GS). The surge in pay came as Jane Street generated about $39.6 billion in trading revenue last year, a trading haul that put the firm ahead of major Wall Street banks and market-making rivals. Warning! Behind that payout is a much bigger story about scale, capital, and the changing structure of modern trading. Jane Street's members' equity, which gives the firm firepower to support its trading operations without relying on outside capital, has climbed nearly 2,000% since 2016 to $45 billion, according to the people familiar with the matter. That capital base could be helping the firm take advantage of market swings, expand across assets from stocks to corporate bonds and exchange-traded funds, and put money into startups at earlier stages. Jane Street has also benefited from loans and bonds issued in the public debt market, while placing bets on companies including Anthropic, which has received offers for a new funding round that would value the AI startup at about $800 billion or more. The firm is also in funding talks for Fluidstack and recently invested an additional $1 billion in CoreWeave (NASDAQ:CRWV). For investors, Jane Street's rise could be a powerful signal of how much Wall Street's center of gravity has shifted toward algorithm-driven market makers with deep internal capital bases. The firm has kept growing despite recent scrutiny, including accusations from regulators in India in July that Jane Street manipulated markets while running what had once been one of its most lucrative trading strategies, allegations the firm has denied. Jane Street has also asked a judge to dismiss a separate lawsuit accusing it of trading on inside information ahead of the $40 billion crash of cryptocurrencies tied to Terraform Labs. Even with those challenges, the firm's 2025 trading haul topped Citadel Securities, which generated a firm-record $12.2 billion in trading revenue last year, and Jane Street is preparing for more expansion after recently placing an offer to lease a new London office that would double its footprint in the UK capital.
