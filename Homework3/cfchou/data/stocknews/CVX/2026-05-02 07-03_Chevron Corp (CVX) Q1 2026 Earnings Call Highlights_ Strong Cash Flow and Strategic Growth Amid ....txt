Chevron Corp (NYSE:CVX) delivered solid performance with disciplined execution and a resilient portfolio despite market volatility and geopolitical tensions. US production exceeded 2 million barrels of oil equivalent per day, with Gorgon and Wheatstone LNG running at full rates. Chevron's high-quality upstream and downstream portfolios delivered significant integration benefits, maximizing margins across products. The company announced an asset swap in Venezuela, increasing its position in the Orinoco region, which offers long-term growth potential. Chevron generated cash flow from operations of $7.1 billion in the quarter, with adjusted free cash flow at $4.1 billion. Chevron reported a $360 million charge related to legal reserves, impacting earnings. Foreign currency effects decreased earnings by $223 million. Adjusted downstream earnings decreased due to unfavorable timing effects, reflecting a steep rise in commodity prices. Working capital was impacted by sharp commodity price increases and inventory build-up. The conflict in the Middle East had a limited impact on production, with less than 5% of the portfolio located in the region. Q: Mike, how do you view the current conflict in the Middle East and its implications for Chevron's mid-cycle pricing assumptions?A: Michael Wirth, CEO: The conflict is a significant disruption to the global energy system. While it's too early to predict long-term changes, Chevron remains committed to capital and cost discipline, investing in competitive assets, and maintaining a strong balance sheet. Our strategy is consistent, and we will adapt as the situation evolves. Q: Can you discuss the value capture opportunities from Chevron's refining system and increased exposure to waterborne crudes post-Hess merger?A: Michael Wirth, CEO: We've established a global enterprise optimization team to maximize value across our assets. Our refineries are running at high utilization, capturing good margins. In Asia, we expect over 40% Chevron equity crude throughput in Q2, and over 50% in the US, allowing us to capture margins effectively. Q: What is Chevron's approach to capital allocation amid higher prices, and what would prompt increased spending in the Permian?A: Eimear Bonner, CFO: We remain consistent with our financial priorities: growing the dividend, investing efficiently, maintaining a strong balance sheet, and conducting share buybacks. We are not changing our capital allocation framework or ranges. It's too early to make changes based on the current conflict. Q: Are there plans to increase capital investment in Venezuela or the Permian given the current market conditions?A: Michael Wirth, CEO: In Venezuela, we are still in debt recovery mode and need further progress before increasing capital investment. In the Permian, we focus on strong free cash flow and asset reliability. We could increase production, but current conditions don't warrant immediate changes. Q: Can you provide an update on the exclusivity agreement with Microsoft for power projects?A: Michael Wirth, CEO: We are in exclusive discussions with Microsoft for a power project in West Texas. The project is progressing well, with permits submitted and equipment secured. We aim for FID later this year, balancing power price expectations with investment returns.
