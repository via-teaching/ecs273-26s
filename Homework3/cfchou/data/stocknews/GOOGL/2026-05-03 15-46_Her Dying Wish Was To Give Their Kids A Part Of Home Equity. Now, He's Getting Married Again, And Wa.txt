As his wife battled ovarian cancer, she made a request that felt meaningful and urgent: she wanted to leave a legacyfor their children. So he agreed. In the middle of what he described as an “all hands on deck” period, he signed paperwork that gave each of their two kids 25% ownership of their fully paid-off home, worth around $750,000 to $800,000. The house is being preparedfor sale, and each child stands to receive roughly $175,000 to $200,000. Jeff, who is getting married again and planning a move to Atlanta, said his concern isn’t about the money itself, but about what it could do to them. “I feel like it’s teaching them the wrong message,” he said. “They’re getting a windfall at a very young age.” He wants to roll the equity from the home into a new property with his future wife. That has led him to consider asking his kids to sign their shares back over to him. But there’s a problem: they don’t want to.
