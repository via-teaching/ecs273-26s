Seaport Research, Truist, Baird, and JPMorgan each lifted their price targets on Amphenol, signaling increased confidence in the company’s execution and overall setup. The cluster of target increases on 30 April suggests these firms see room between prior expectations and where they think the shares can reasonably trade. This supports a firmer valuation anchor for investors tracking Street views. Jefferies recently assumed coverage with a Buy rating following a prior Hold stance, reinforcing the idea that some analysts are more comfortable with Amphenol’s risk and reward trade off at current levels. UBS and Citi each lowered their price targets in April, indicating pockets of caution on assumptions around growth, margins, or both, even as other firms turned more constructive. Evercore ISI removed Amphenol from its "Tactical Outperform" list after Nvidia’s GTC commentary on copper and co packaged optics. This suggests some concern that the evolving copper versus optics roadmap could cap upside in the near term despite an Outperform rating and a US$165 target.
