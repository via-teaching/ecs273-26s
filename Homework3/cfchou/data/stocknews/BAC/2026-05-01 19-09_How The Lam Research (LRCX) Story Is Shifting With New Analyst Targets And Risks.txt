Lam Research sits in the spotlight after a series of analyst price target changes, with individual moves ranging from US$6 to US$105 per share. These shifts come as research firms reassess wafer fab equipment demand, earnings potential and how much investors might be willing to pay. This is highlighted by actions such as BofA adding Lam Research to its US 1 List, while others maintain more cautious stances. BofA highlighted Lam Research by adding the stock to its US 1 List, grouping it with what the firm describes as its best investment ideas. This signals strong confidence in execution and long term earnings potential. Multiple firms, including Morgan Stanley, Barclays, Goldman Sachs, Citi, Susquehanna, Oppenheimer, TD Cowen, Stifel, B. Riley, Deutsche Bank and Argus, raised their Lam Research price targets, reflecting more constructive views on wafer fab equipment demand, especially around DRAM and advanced packaging. Barclays and Morgan Stanley referenced a robust investment environment for wafer fab equipment and updated market growth forecasts. This supports the case that Lam Research could benefit if those industry expectations play out. Erste Group downgraded Lam Research, suggesting some concern that execution, valuation or industry expectations may already embed a lot of optimism even as other firms raise their targets. Several firms, such as Morgan Stanley and Barclays, kept Equal Weight type ratings alongside higher targets. For these analysts, risk and reward appear more balanced than outright bullish. Reports indicated that BE Semiconductor is reviewing takeover interest from potential buyers, with Lam Research cited among possible suitors alongside Applied Materials, which already holds a 9% stake in BE Semi. Media coverage pointed to communication between Elon Musk's team and chip suppliers around a Terafab concept, highlighting ongoing attention on high performance computing and the related chip supply chain.
