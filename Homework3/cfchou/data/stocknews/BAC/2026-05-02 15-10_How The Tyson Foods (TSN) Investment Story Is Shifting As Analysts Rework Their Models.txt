Piper Sandler recently upgraded Tyson Foods to Overweight, pointing to sales momentum as a key pillar of its constructive view on the shares. Mizuho initiated coverage with a bullish stance, which adds another supportive voice around the company’s growth prospects and execution potential. Several firms, including BofA, JPMorgan, Bernstein, BMO, and Barclays, raised their Tyson price targets on 3 February 2026, signaling a more optimistic stance on valuation after reviewing their models. The U.S. Department of Justice antitrust division is reported to be running a criminal investigation into major meatpackers, with Tyson Foods among the publicly traded companies under scrutiny, according to the Wall Street Journal. Reports indicate the DOJ probe is focused on beef companies, including large publicly traded processors such as Tyson Foods, as part of a broader review of competition in the meat sector. Senate Democrats are preparing legislation that would break up large U.S. meatpacking companies. The proposal could prevent firms from processing more than one type of meat and may require processors like Tyson Foods to spin off beef plants. Tyson Foods launched a new Jimmy Dean high protein frozen lineup, featuring bowls with 40 grams of protein, sandwiches with 25 grams, and waffles with 20 grams, now available in various flavors at select retailers nationwide.
