Delta Air Lines (DAL,Financials) is cutting back on itsgoals for growth since jet fuel prices have goneup sharply. This shows that airline margins are under more and more pressure.The company announced it will cancel all planned capacity growth for the June quarter. This means that supply will be around 3.5 percentage points lower than what was expected. Fuel prices have almost doubled since the end of February because of political tensions in the Middle East.Delta thinks it will pay roughly $4.30 per gallon for jet fuel in the second quarter. This will add more than $2 billion to its fuel costs compared to the same time last year. Because of this, the airline's outlook for adjusted earnings per share was $1.00 to $1.50, which is less than what analysts had expected.Delta noted that demand is still robust, especially among travelers with better incomes, and ticket sales have continued to climb at a rate of more than 10% each year. The airline wants to pay some of the higher expenses by raising ticket prices and other levies. They hope to make up for up to half of the extra fuel costs.The company also said that its adjusted earnings for the first quarter were 64 cents per share, which was better than expected.Investors will keep an eye on how long gasoline costs stay high and if airlines can stay profitable without hurting demand too much.
