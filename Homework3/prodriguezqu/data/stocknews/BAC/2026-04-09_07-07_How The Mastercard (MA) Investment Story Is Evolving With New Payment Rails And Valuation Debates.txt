Mastercard's modeled fair value has been trimmed slightly, moving from US$662.59 to US$657.11, signaling only a small adjustment in the latest price target work. That fine tuning lines up with research that is generally constructive but split between more bullish views on electronic payments growth and more cautious takes on valuation, regulation, and new payment rails. As you read on, you will see how these shifting inputs shape the current analyst narrative and what to watch to stay on top of it.
Analyst Price Targets don't always capture the full story. Head over to our Company Report to find new ways to value Mastercard.
BofA reinstated Mastercard with a Buy rating and a US$700 price target, and describes the card networks as some of the strongest risk adjusted and more defensive opportunities within its payments coverage.
Compass Point upgraded Mastercard to Buy from Neutral and lifted its price target to US$735 from US$620, pointing to what it sees as strong net revenue potential and a valuation that it views as too low.
Evercore ISI highlights the BVNK acquisition as both an offensive and defensive move, aiming to link traditional card rails with blockchain settlement, which it argues addresses concerns about new payment technologies.
Truist reports that buy side investors in its survey frequently cited Mastercard among the names they would be inclined to defend in financials, grouping it with other large, liquid franchises.
Several firms, including Evercore ISI, UBS, JPMorgan, Raymond James, Rothschild & Co Redburn and BofA, have trimmed Mastercard price targets in recent months, reflecting more conservative assumptions on what they are willing to pay for the stock.

Mastercard launched a Crypto Partner Program that brings together more than 85 digital asset and payments firms, including Binance, Circle, Ripple, Gemini, PayPal and Paxos, to link blockchain technology with existing global commerce infrastructure.
The company is considering the sale of its Nets payments unit, a business that generates about US$370 million in annual revenue and was originally acquired to expand beyond traditional card payments.
Modern Treasury joined the Mastercard Crypto Partner Program as an on and off ramp provider, working with Mastercard and other participants to support fiat to crypto and crypto to fiat payment flows using Mastercard Move Cross Border Services.
SoFi and Mastercard expanded their relationship so that SoFiUSD, a fully reserved U.S. dollar stablecoin, can be used as a settlement option on Mastercard’s network, with plans to explore card settlement, remittances and other stablecoin based money movement.
