Coca-Cola has replaced Pepsi as Marriott International's global beverage provider.
The multi year agreement covers nearly 9,700 hotels worldwide across Marriott's portfolio.
The partnership gives Coca-Cola exposure to millions of hotel guests around the world.
Coca-Cola (NYSE: KO) is entering this new agreement with Marriott from a position of scale and brand recognition, supported by a broad beverage portfolio. The shares recently closed at $77.29, with a return of 11.8% year to date and 69.0% over 5 years, which frames how the market has treated the stock over different time horizons. For investors watching the consumer staples space, this hotel deal adds another data point on how a large brand can secure high traffic channels.
Looking ahead, readers may want to watch how this Marriott partnership influences Coca-Cola's visibility with international travelers and corporate clients. The agreement also matters in the context of the long running rivalry with Pepsi, as it shifts a long standing hotel pouring relationship to Coca-Cola across thousands of properties.
