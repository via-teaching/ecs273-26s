Whether you are wondering if Coca-Cola at around US$77.22 a share is still a solid deal or starting to look expensive, this breakdown will help you frame what that price might actually represent.
The stock has returned 1.2% over the last 7 days, 0.2% over the last 30 days, 11.7% year to date and 16.2% over the past year. This raises fair questions about whether recent performance is already reflected in the price.
Recent coverage has continued to focus on Coca-Cola as a major global beverages brand, with attention on how it manages pricing, costs and product mix in a competitive market. This context helps explain why investors keep reassessing what they are willing to pay for its shares.

A Discounted Cash Flow, or DCF, model projects a company’s future cash flows and then discounts them back to today’s dollars to estimate what the business might be worth now.
For Coca-Cola, the model uses a 2 Stage Free Cash Flow to Equity approach. The latest twelve month free cash flow is about $5.35b. Analyst estimates and further extrapolations by Simply Wall St project free cash flow reaching about $15.27b in 2030, with a series of annual forecasts in between that are discounted back to today using a required return on equity.
When the projected cash flows from 2026 through 2035 are discounted and combined, the model arrives at an estimated intrinsic value of about $87.69 per share. Compared with the current share price of around $77.22, this implies Coca-Cola trades at an 11.9% discount to this DCF estimate, which indicates the shares are undervalued on these assumptions.
Result: UNDERVALUED

For a profitable business like Coca-Cola, the P/E ratio is a useful way to think about what you are paying for each dollar of current earnings. It combines the market’s view on growth prospects and risk into a single number that is easy to compare across companies.
In general, higher expected growth and lower perceived risk can support a higher “normal” or “fair” P/E, while slower expected growth or higher risk usually call for a lower one. Coca-Cola currently trades on a P/E of about 25.36x. That sits above the Beverage industry average of roughly 16.74x, and modestly above a peer group average of about 24.09x.
Simply Wall St’s Fair Ratio for Coca-Cola is 26.26x. This is a proprietary estimate of what the P/E might be given factors such as earnings growth, profit margins, industry, market cap and key risks. Because it is tailored to the company’s own profile, it can be more informative than a simple comparison with peers or the broad industry. With the current P/E slightly below the Fair Ratio, the shares appear modestly undervalued on this multiple-based framework.
Result: UNDERVALUED
