A Discounted Cash Flow model estimates what a company might be worth by projecting its future cash flows and then discounting those back to today using a required rate of return. It is essentially a way of asking what those future dollars are worth in present terms.
Caterpillar generated trailing twelve month free cash flow of about US$8.1b. Based on analyst inputs and further projections, Simply Wall St models free cash flow rising to US$16.3b in 2030, with a series of annual forecasts between now and then. Projections beyond the first few analyst covered years are extrapolated using the 2 Stage Free Cash Flow to Equity approach, which tapers growth over time.
Adding up all those discounted cash flows produces an estimated intrinsic value of about US$632.64 per share. Compared to the recent share price of US$721.24, the model implies Caterpillar trades at roughly a 14.0% premium to this DCF estimate, which in this analysis suggests the shares are overvalued on this specific method.
Result: OVERVALUED

For profitable companies like Caterpillar, the P/E ratio is a straightforward way to connect what you pay today with the earnings the business is currently generating. It shows how many dollars investors are willing to pay for each dollar of earnings.
What counts as a "normal" or "fair" P/E depends on how the market views a company’s growth prospects and risk. Higher expected growth or lower perceived risk can justify a higher P/E, while lower growth or higher risk usually means a lower one.
Caterpillar currently trades on a P/E of 37.77x. That is above both the Machinery industry average P/E of 26.21x and a peer group average of 26.44x, which indicates that investors are currently paying more per dollar of earnings than for many similar companies.
Simply Wall St’s Fair Ratio for Caterpillar is 45.78x. This is a proprietary estimate of what the P/E might be, based on factors such as earnings growth, profit margins, industry, market cap and specific risks. Because it blends these company-specific inputs, it can provide a more tailored reference point than a simple comparison with peers or the broad industry.
Comparing the Fair Ratio of 45.78x with the current P/E of 37.77x indicates that the shares are trading below that customised benchmark on this metric.
Result: UNDERVALUED
