Abbott Laboratories is back in focus as fresh research feeds into updated price targets, with several firms clustering their revisions in a short span. Some are trimming targets while others lean toward levels around the mid US$150s, which reflects an ongoing debate about how much you should be willing to pay for Abbott given the current earnings outlook. As you read on, you will see how these shifting targets fit into a broader analyst narrative and how to keep track of where sentiment is moving next.

Several firms, including Goldman Sachs and Citi, maintain positive ratings on Abbott while fine tuning targets, signaling that they still see the business as fundamentally solid even as they recalibrate their models.
Bernstein recently lifted its target to US$154, highlighting what it views as healthy sector fundamentals in medical technology and continued investor interest in larger healthcare names such as Abbott.
A broad group of banks, including Barclays, Evercore ISI, Wells Fargo, Oppenheimer, Citi, Mizuho, Piper Sandler and BTIG, has cut price targets in recent months, which points to increased scrutiny on what investors are paying for current earnings and pipeline execution.
Multiple US$10 plus target reductions on a single day in January from firms such as Bernstein, Wells Fargo and others show that some analysts are reassessing growth assumptions and the risk around Abbott delivering on its plans, even if ratings often remain constructive.

Abbott and Flatiron Health are integrating Abbott's Precision Oncology tests, including Oncotype DX Breast Recurrence Score and OncoExTra, directly into Flatiron's OncoEMR. This will allow more than 1,600 U.S. community cancer centers and 4,700 providers to order and receive results within their existing workflow.
New FreeDM2 trial data show people with Type 2 diabetes using Abbott's FreeStyle Libre continuous glucose monitoring system had a 0.6% greater HbA1c reduction and spent 2.5 more hours per day in a healthy glucose range compared with traditional fingersticks. Italian real world data also point to better average glucose levels and quality of life.
Abbott reported 12 month data for its Volt Pulsed Field Ablation System and six month data for its TactiFlex Duo Ablation Catheter. The results included 84.2% freedom from rhythm recurrence in paroxysmal AFib for Volt and an 81% success rate in paroxysmal AFib cases for TactiFlex Duo, alongside FDA approval, CE Mark and Breakthrough Device Designation in related programs.
A wrongful death lawsuit has been filed against Abbott over alleged defects in FreeStyle Libre 3 and FreeStyle Libre 3 Plus sensors. The lawsuit cites links to seven deaths globally, about 800 reports of serious injury and a prior medical device correction affecting about 3,000,000 sensors in the U.S., following earlier FDA classified recalls tied to accuracy issues.
