The redemption spike in Goldman Sachs Private Credit Corp. gives you a rare, real time look at investor behavior in a segment that typically moves quietly. Requests of 4.999% of outstanding shares show that some investors are taking risk off the table, but the fact that all withdrawals were met, alongside roughly US$1.04b of gross subscriptions in Q1, points to a fund that is still attracting capital. For a bank that competes with Blackstone, KKR and Apollo in private credit, being able to meet redemptions when some peers have gated flows can matter for reputation and future fundraising. The emphasis on institutional clients, rather than shorter term retail money, also speaks to the type of fee income Goldman Sachs is trying to build, which may be relevant if you care about how stable non trading revenues are through the credit cycle.
